How to make Kombucha

Equipment

  • 3L glass jar
  • Filter cloth
  • Tea strainer
  • Rubber band or rope
  • Stirring spoon
  • Food thermometer
  • pH paper

Ingredients

  • 1 liter Drink water
  • 4 g. coffee leaves
  • 100 g sugar
  • 100 ml starter liquid
  • 1 SCOBY

Steps

  1. Cleaning the Equipment: Sterilize glass bottles or jars by thoroughly washing and scalding them with boiling water for 1-2 minutes. Consider using steaming or baking as alternatives.
  2. Making Starter Tea: Bring the water to a boil. Then, add the tea, cover, and turn off the heat. Set aside for about 10 minutes. Filter out the tea leaves and put the tea into the prepared glass jar. The water used for fermentation must be potable or chlorine-free filtered water.
  3. Mixing Sugar in the Starter Tea: Pour sugar into a glass jar and use stirring spoon to stir until completely dissolved. Let the tea cool completely before next step. Make sure the tea has cooled down to room temperature. Anything above 35°C will harm the SCOBY.
  4. Adding Starter and SCOBY into the Tea Infusion: Put Kombucha starter and SCOBY in the jar. Cover the jar with a filter cloth, tie it tightly, and place it in a ventilated area. Check the pH level of the fermented tea, which should be around 3.3-3.5 or lower than 4 to prevent mold growth. Avoid moving the jar. Leave at least 5 cm at the top for fermentation activity and SCOBY growth.
  5. Fermentation Time: Ferment your kombucha for 7-14-21 days until you achieve the desired taste. Pour out the kombucha water, filter it, and transfer it into a bottle for storage in the refrigerator. For a sweeter taste, ferment for a shorter period. For a sour taste, extend the fermentation period.
